Three world-class birding theatres, one comfortable base

Salinas & corrales

Bay of Cádiz salt-pans and the intertidal corrales: wintering waders, Osprey, flamingos, Spoonbill — drive-up, car-as-hide photography.

Bonanza salinas and the Sanlúcar boat — the great wetland's birds, without the permits or the queues.

Gibraltar's raptor bottleneck — thousands of eagles, kites and vultures funnelling between two continents.

Featured site · A living hide

Bird the corrales — the best natural hide on the coast

The corrales are semicircular stone weirs raised on the intertidal zone, a fishing art practised in Rota since at least 1399 and Andalusia's first Natural Monument. As the tide falls, they become a stage of glistening flats where gulls, terns and waders feed within yards of the wall.

Time your visit to a falling tide and Audouin's Gulls loaf on the stones while Sandwich Terns plunge the channels.

Every guided low-tide visit directly supports the corraleros who keep these 2,000-year-old structures standing — and the habitat that more than a hundred bird species depend on.

Where to go

Best birding sites around Rota

01–02

Río Salado & pinewoods

Tidal estuary on the town edge for waders and flamingos; umbrella pine for Azure-winged Magpie, Hoopoe and owls.

03–04

Bay salinas & Bonanza

10,500 ha of marismas — the wader, flamingo and Osprey engine room — and the province's premier wader site on Doñana's edge.

05–07

Chipiona, Medina & the Strait

Spain's largest Little Swift colony, a freshwater laguna for White-headed Duck, and Tarifa's raptor funnel on a day trip.

Good to know

Frequently asked

When is the best time to visit?

October–March: peak wader numbers, wintering Osprey and flamingos, plus passage raptors — at off-season prices.

Do I need a car?

Recommended — it doubles as a hide on the salinas. Many sites are also walkable or cyclable from town.

Do I need permits?

Not for the local salinas, coast, pinewoods or corrales. Core Doñana 4×4 routes are guided/permit-based — we can arrange them.

Can I hire a local guide?

Yes — we connect you with vetted local experts, and the corrales are visited guided, by the tide calendar.

Is English spoken?

Widely, along the coast and in our partner hotels. Guided visits run in Spanish and English.

Is it good for photographers?

Excellent — drive-up wader photography and golden-hour flamingos, with soft winter light.
